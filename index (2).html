The History of Mumbai

From Seven Islands to the City of Dreams

1924

Gateway of India

Built to commemorate the visit of King George V. This arch stands as a silent sentinel of the city's colonial past and its transition into a modern global hub.

1930s

Marine Drive

Part of the Back Bay Reclamation, this "Queen's Necklace" redefined Mumbai's shoreline with its iconic Art Deco curve and sunset views.

1878

CSM Terminus

A UNESCO World Heritage site, this masterpiece of Victorian Gothic architecture serves as the beating heart of India's railway network.

1534

Elephanta Caves

Hidden on a quiet island, these ancient rock-cut temples dedicated to Lord Shiva represent the artistic peak of Indian sculpture.

1890s

Juhu Beach

Once a quiet edge of the city, Juhu evolved from Jamsetji Tata's vision into Mumbai's most famous coastal retreat.

1801

Siddhivinayak Temple

What started as a small structure commissioned by Deubai Patil is now one of the most visited spiritual landmarks in the world.
